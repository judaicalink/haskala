# Getting started

The Library of the Haskala is a research database for books from the
Jewish Enlightenment period and the people, places, publishers, series,
topics and occupations that surround them. This page explains how to
find your way around.

## Home page

The home page hosts the project description and the entry points into
each of the major lists. Use the navigation bar at the top of every
page to move between them at any time.

## Browse lists

Each main entity has its own alphabetic index:

- **Books** — every catalogued title.
- **Digital books** — the subset of books with a public digital copy
  (image scan or transcript).
- **Persons** — authors, editors, translators, printers, subscribers
  and other people who appear in the catalogue.
- **Places** — cities of publication, birth, death, and mention.
- **Publishers**, **Series**, **Topics**, **Occupations** —
  controlled vocabularies that link records together.

Each index opens with a count, a Latin and (where relevant) a Hebrew
alphabet jump nav, and one section per first letter. Click an item to
open its detail page.

## Search

The header search field accepts a free-text query and runs across book
titles, person names and city names. The result page exposes filters
for year range, language, place and whether a digital copy exists.

## Detail pages

Books, persons and places each have a polished detail page with a
sticky table of contents. See
[detail-pages.md](detail-pages.md) for the anatomy of each.

## Exporting citations

Every book detail page has a **Cite** button that opens a modal with a
ready-to-copy plain citation and downloads for BibTeX and RIS. See
[citations.md](citations.md).

## Accessibility and availability

- All detail pages are keyboard-navigable. The sticky TOC mirrors
  document headings so you can jump straight to a section.
- On small screens the TOC collapses into a floating **Sections**
  button at the bottom-left of the page.
- The dataset is published under CC-BY 4.0; you may quote and
  redistribute records as long as you credit the project.
